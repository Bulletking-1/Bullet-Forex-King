Smart Money Concepts (SMC)

Smart Money Concepts is about understanding how the largest market participants — banks, hedge funds, and institutions — manipulate price to capture liquidity and maximize profits. Retail traders often get trapped, while institutions quietly build their positions.

By studying SMC, you learn to identify:

  • Liquidity Grabs: Stop hunts that trap retail traders.
  • Fair Value Gaps (FVGs): Imbalances in price action that signal institutional moves.
  • Premium & Discount Zones: Where price is overvalued or undervalued.
  • Mitigation Blocks: Levels where institutions rebalance their positions.
"The market is designed to transfer wealth from the impatient to the patient."

Liquidity

Liquidity is the fuel of the forex market. Institutions require liquidity to fill their massive orders. They often create fake breakouts or push the market into retail stop-loss zones to capture this liquidity.

Understanding liquidity allows traders to anticipate:

  • Where retail stop-losses are likely placed.
  • Why false breakouts occur before big moves.
  • How institutions engineer liquidity grabs before the trend continues.
"Liquidity is not random — it’s the lifeblood of institutional trading."

Institutional Order Flow

Order flow is the trail left behind by institutional players. It shows where smart money is accumulating or distributing positions. Learning to read order flow helps traders align with the true market direction.

Key principles of order flow include:

  • Accumulation: Quiet buying before a major bullish move.
  • Distribution: Quiet selling before a major bearish move.
  • Break of Structure (BOS): Confirmation of institutional intent.
  • Change of Character (ChoCH): The early sign of a trend reversal.
"Follow the footprints of smart money — they always leave a trail."
