Liquidity Pools & Sweeps

Liquidity pools are price levels where a large cluster of stop orders sitâ€”commonly around obvious swing highs and lows. Institutions target these pools to generate the volume needed for large moves.

Advanced Insight: Look for equal highs/lows that get taken out with momentum but quickly reverseâ€”this indicates a liquidity grab. The subsequent move often targets the opposite side's liquidity.
Trading Tip: When price aggressively taps a liquidity pool and immediately rejects, it often signals a liquidity grab. Combine with a structure break (BOS/CHoCH) for higher-probability entries.

Order Blocks & Mitigation

An order block is the final bullish/bearish candle before a strong impulsive move. These zones mark areas where institutions placed significant orders. Mitigation occurs when price returns to these blocks to "fill" remaining orders.

Advanced Insight: The most powerful order blocks form after a liquidity sweep. The mitigation block often provides the highest probability entry for the next impulsive move.
Trading Tip: After a sharp move, price frequently revisits the origin candle (order block) to mitigate institutional orders. Look for rejection wicks, engulfing patterns, or LTF BOS as confirmation.

Market Structure Shifts

Structure is the backbone of analysis: sequences of HH/HL denote bullish conditions; LH/LL denote bearish conditions. A Break of Structure (BOS) confirms momentum, while a Change of Character (CHoCH) signals potential reversal.

Advanced Insight: The first pullback after a BOS often creates the most efficient order block. A CHoCH that occurs after a liquidity sweep is a high-probability reversal signal.
Trading Tip: Align with the higher-timeframe trend. If the Daily is bullish (HH+HL), seek longs; use lower-timeframe CHoCH/BOS to trigger entries.

Supply & Demand Zones

Zones form where sharp moves leave imbalancesâ€”revealing where buyers or sellers dominated. These zones become future targets for price returns.

Advanced Insight: The strongest zones form after a liquidity sweep and BOS. These zones often align with institutional order blocks and previous daily/weekly highs/lows.
Trading Tip: In bullish trends, buy at demand; in bearish trends, sell at supply. Wait for price confirmation (candlesticks or structure shifts).

Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

FVGs are three-candle imbalances where the first and third candles don't overlap, leaving an inefficiency that price tends to rebalance. These act as mini supply/demand zones.

Advanced Insight: FVGs that form after a liquidity sweep and BOS often become the "roadmap" for price, acting as support in bullish trends and resistance in bearish trends.
Trading Tip: Mark FVGs on 1H/4H. When price retraces into the gap and rejects, continuation is likelyâ€”especially when aligned with OBs and HTF bias.

Mitigation Blocks & Entries

When price revisits a prior order block to close remaining institutional orders, the level acts as a mitigation blockâ€”often giving precise entries with minimal risk.

Advanced Insight: The best mitigation entries occur when a liquidity sweep happens first, then price returns to the mitigation block. This often creates a "liquidity vacuum" that propels price.
Trading Tip: Look for mitigation at previously violated OBs, ideally paired with a liquidity sweep and BOS/CHoCH confluence.
