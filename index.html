Smart Money Concepts

Liquidity Pools

Liquidity pools are price levels where a large cluster of stop orders sit—commonly around obvious swing highs and lows. Institutions target these pools to generate the volume needed for large moves.

Trading Tip: When price aggressively taps a liquidity pool and immediately rejects, it often signals a liquidity grab. Combine with a structure break (BOS/CHoCH) for higher-probability entries.

Order Blocks

An order block is the final bullish/bearish candle before a strong impulsive move. These zones mark areas where institutions placed significant orders.

Trading Tip: After a sharp move, price frequently revisits the origin candle (order block) to mitigate institutional orders. Look for rejection wicks, engulfing patterns, or LTF BOS as confirmation.

Market Structure

Structure is the backbone of analysis: sequences of HH/HL denote bullish conditions; LH/LL denote bearish conditions.

Trading Tip: Align with the higher-timeframe trend. If the Daily is bullish (HH+HL), seek longs; use lower-timeframe CHoCH/BOS to trigger entries.

Supply & Demand Zones

Zones form where sharp moves leave imbalances—revealing where buyers or sellers dominated.

Trading Tip: In bullish trends, buy at demand; in bearish trends, sell at supply. Wait for price confirmation (candlesticks or structure shifts).

Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

FVGs are three-candle imbalances where the first and third candles don’t overlap, leaving an inefficiency that price tends to rebalance.

Trading Tip: Mark FVGs on 1H/4H. When price retraces into the gap and rejects, continuation is likely—especially when aligned with OBs and HTF bias.

Mitigation Blocks

When price revisits a prior order block to close remaining institutional orders, the level acts as a mitigation block—often giving precise entries.

Trading Tip: Look for mitigation at previously violated OBs, ideally paired with a liquidity sweep and BOS/CHoCH confluence.
