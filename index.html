Smart Money Concepts

Learn how institutional traders approach the market with these detailed explanations:

Liquidity Pools

Liquidity pools are areas on the chart where a large number of buy or sell orders are clustered. These often appear at previous highs and lows where traders have placed their stop losses.

How to trade liquidity: Smart money often pushes price to these liquidity pools to trigger stops before reversing. Look for price to sweep these levels then show reversal patterns like engulfing candles or divergence.

Order Blocks

Order blocks are areas where large institutional orders have been placed, creating an imbalance between buyers and sellers. These appear as strong, impulsive candles on the chart.

How to trade order blocks: After a strong move, price will often retrace back to the order block area before continuing in the original direction. Enter when price returns to these zones and shows signs of rejection.

Market Structure

Market structure refers to the pattern of highs and lows that define the trend. Break of structure (BOS) and change of character (CHoCH) are key concepts in Smart Money Concepts.

How to read market structure: In an uptrust, we look for higher highs (HH) and higher lows (HL). A break below the last HL signals a possible trend change. The opposite is true for downtrends.

Supply & Demand Zones

Supply and demand zones are areas where price has previously made a strong move, leaving an imbalance between buyers and sellers.

How to trade supply/demand: Look for strong moves away from these zones. When price returns to a demand zone, look for buying opportunities. When price returns to a supply zone, look for selling opportunities.

Fair Value Gaps

Fair Value Gaps (FVG) are three-candle patterns that create an imbalance or gap in the market. These often act as support or resistance.

How to trade FVGs: After a strong move, price will often retrace back to fill the FVG before continuing in the original direction. These can be used as entry zones or for setting profit targets.

Mitigation Blocks

Mitigation blocks are order blocks that have been partially filled. Price often returns to these blocks to "mitigate" or complete the remaining orders.

How to trade mitigation blocks: These are similar to order blocks but often provide even higher probability setups. Look for price to return to these areas and show rejection candles before entering in the direction of the original move.
